Home & Instructions

Welcome to the Klipper Calibration web application!

This tool is a web-based version of the popular Klipper Calibration spreadsheet, designed to be easier to use without needing to make a copy.

Select a calculator from the sidebar on the left to get started. All calculations are based on the fantastic guides from the OrcaSlicer Wiki and Ellis' Print Tuning Guide.

How to Use

  1. Select a calibration tool from the sidebar menu.
  2. Enter your measured values into the yellow input fields.
  3. Your results will be calculated automatically and shown in the green result boxes.
  4. Follow the instructions and copy any generated commands to your Klipper console.

Basic Configuration Checks

A selection of recommended configuration checks to do on the first boot of Klipper.

Process Commands (Use in Console) Notes
Verify temperature Look at the interface. The displayed temperatures for the nozzle and bed should be around ambient room temperature.
Verify M112 M112 This will put the printer into a shutdown state.
Restart Firmware FIRMWARE_RESTART Reboot the printer. Verify temps are staying at ambient and not rising. If they are rising, Power Off! Check wiring and printer.cfg.
Verify heaters Set extruder temperature to 50°C. Watch the graph; it should start to rise within 30 seconds. If not, verify heater_pin. Turn off the heater and repeat for the heated bed (e.g., to 60°C). Verify it heats and cools.
Verify fans M106 S255 (Part Fan) Turns on the print cooling fan to 100%. Verify it turns on.
M106 S0 Turns off the print cooling fan.
SET_HEATER_FAN_SPEED HEATER=hotend_fan SPEED=1 (If you have a [heater_fan]) Turns on the hotend fan. Verify it turns on. Use SPEED=0 to turn off.
Verify stepper motors G28 (Home All) Keep a hand on the power switch! Verify all axes move in the correct direction towards their endstops. If an axis moves the wrong way, E-Stop or power off! You will need to invert the dir_pin for that axis (add or remove a !) in your printer.cfg.
Verify extruder motor Heat the hotend to printing temp (e.g., 200°C). Once at temp, go to the console and click "Extrude". Verify the extruder motor turns in the correct direction (pushing filament towards the nozzle). If not, verify the dir_pin for the extruder.
Calibrate PID settings PID_CALIBRATE HEATER=extruder TARGET=220 Calibrate the extruder PID. Use your typical printing temperature.
PID_CALIBRATE HEATER=heater_bed TARGET=60 Caligate the bed PID. Use your typical bed temperature.
Save Configuration SAVE_CONFIG After PID tuning is complete, run this to save the new values to your printer.cfg.

Max Volumetric Speed (Ellis' Method)

Ellis' Max Volumetric Flow Rate

Reference: Ellis' Print Tuning Guide

This method finds the maximum speed your extruder can push filament before it starts slipping or grinding.

  1. Heat the printer to your normal printing temperature.
  2. Mark your filament at 120mm before it enters the extruder.
  3. Open your Klipper console.
  4. Send M83 to enter relative extrusion mode.
  5. Send G1 E100 F300. This attempts to extrude 100mm at 5mm/s (F300 = 5mm/s * 60).
  6. When it finishes, measure the distance from the extruder to your mark.
    • If it is 20mm (120 - 100), your extruder kept up.
    • If it is > 20mm, your extruder skipped.
  7. If it succeeded, increase the F value (e.g., G1 E100 F360) and try again.
  8. Find the highest F value that can successfully extrude 100mm.

Use the F value (speed in mm/min) to find your flow rate in mm/s.

Extrusion Speed (mm/s):

...

Formula: F Value / 60

Max Volumetric Flow (mm³/s):

...

Formula: (Filament Diameter / 2)² * PI * Extrusion Speed

Probe XY Offset

Probe XY Offset Calculator

Reference: Klipper Probe Calibrate Docs

  1. Home your printer.
  2. Run PROBE in the console.
  3. Run GET_POSITION and record the X/Y values below (Probe Position).
  4. Place a mark on the bed exactly under the probe.
  5. Manually jog the nozzle (X and Y) until it is perfectly centered over the mark.
  6. Run GET_POSITION again and record the X/Y values below (Nozzle Position).
